UNIT II OVERVIEW
============================================================

. System simulation is the process of creating a model of a real system
  and experimenting with that model to understand how the system behaves.

. A system may be simple or complex.

. A system may be physical, mathematical, social, economic, biological,
  industrial, or computer-based.

. In simulation, we do not directly experiment with the real system.

. Instead, we create a model of the system and test different conditions.

. Simulation is useful when the real system is:
  . Expensive to test
  . Dangerous to test
  . Difficult to control
  . Too slow to observe
  . Too complex for direct mathematical solution

. System simulation helps to answer questions like:
  . What will happen if demand increases?
  . What will happen if one more server is added?
  . What will happen if production speed is changed?
  . What will happen if arrival rate increases?
  . What will happen if cost or price changes?
  . What is the best decision under uncertainty?

  . Time behavior
  . Randomness
  . Complexity
  . Objective of the study

1. MANUAL SIMULATION
============================================================

. Manual simulation is performed by hand using tables, charts, random
  numbers, and calculations.

. It does not require computer software.

. It is suitable for small and simple problems.

. It helps students understand the basic logic of simulation.

      Example:
      
              . Suppose a small tea shop wants to study customer waiting time.
              
              . We can manually prepare a table with:
                . Customer number
                . Arrival time
                . Service time
                . Service start time
                . Service end time
                . Waiting time
      
            . By calculating these values manually, we can simulate the queue.
      
      Advantages:
      
              . Easy to understand
              
              . Good for learning basic concepts
              
              . No software required
      
      Disadvantages:
      
              . Time-consuming
              
              . Not suitable for large systems
              
              . Error may occur in manual calculation

. Computer simulation uses computer programs to imitate the behavior of

. It is useful for large and complex systems.

. Programming languages or software tools are used.

          Common tools:
          
                          . Python

                          . Excel
                          
                          . R
                          
                          . Simulink
                          
                          . Arena

                          . GPSS

          Example:
          
                  . A hospital wants to simulate patient flow.
                  
                  . Computer simulation can calculate:
                    . Patient arrival time
                    . Waiting time
                    . Doctor utilization
                    . Queue length
                    . Service completion time
          
          Advantages:
          
                    . Fast
                    
                    . Accurate
                    
                    . Can handle complex systems
                    
                    . Can run thousands of experiments
                    
                    . Can produce graphs and reports
          
          Disadvantages:
          
                    . Requires technical skill
                    
                    . Needs valid data

============================================================

. Analog simulation uses physical or electrical systems to represent

. It is based on similarity between two systems.

. In the past, analog computers were used to solve differential
  equations and continuous systems.

          Example:
          
                    . An electrical circuit can be used to represent a mechanical vibration

                    . Water flow in pipes can be used to represent traffic flow.
                    
          Advantages:
          
                    . Useful for continuous systems
                    
                    . Gives real-time behavior
                    
          Disadvantages:

                    . Accuracy may be limited

. It represents system variables using numerical values.

. Most modern simulations are digital simulations.

            Example:

            Disadvantages:

. Hybrid simulation combines analog and digital simulation.

. It uses both physical/analog components and digital computer
  calculation.

            Example:
            
                    . Flight simulator uses:
                      . Physical cockpit controls
                      . Digital computer graphics
                      . Mathematical flight models
                      . Real-time response system
            
            Advantages:
            
                      . Suitable for complex systems
                      
                      . Can combine real-time physical response with digital accuracy
                      
            Disadvantages:

                      . Complex to design

============================================================

. Discrete event simulation studies systems that change at specific
  points in time due to events.

. The state of the system changes only when an event occurs.

            Examples of events:
            
                      . Customer arrives
                      
                      . Customer service starts
                      
                      . Customer service ends
                      
                      . Machine fails
                      
                      . Machine is repaired
                      
                      . Packet arrives in network

                    . Bank queue simulation.
                    
                    . The system changes when:
                      . A customer arrives
                      . A counter becomes free
                      . A customer leaves
            
            Applications:
            
                      . Banking
                      
                      . Hospital queue
                      
                      . Ticket counter
                      
                      . Call center
                      
                      . Computer network
                      
                      . Airport system

============================================================
8. CONTINUOUS SIMULATION
============================================================

. Continuous simulation studies systems that change continuously over
  time.

. The system state changes at every moment.

          Examples:
          
                    . Temperature change
                    
                    . Water level in a tank
                    
                    . Speed of a moving vehicle
                    
                    . Population growth
                    
                    . Disease spread
          
          Example:
          
                  . Water tank system:
                  
                    . Water enters continuously
                    . Water leaves continuously
                    . Water level changes continuously
                  
          Applications:
          
                  . Engineering systems
                  
                  . Biological systems
                  
                  . Weather systems
                  
                  . Chemical processes
                  
                  . Control systems

============================================================
9. DETERMINISTIC SIMULATION
============================================================

. Deterministic simulation has no randomness.

. Same input always gives same output.

          Example:
          
                  . If a machine produces exactly 10 units per hour, then:
                  
                    Production in 8 hours = 10 x 8 = 80 units
                  
                  . Every time we use the same input, the output will be 80 units.
          
          Use:
          
                  . Suitable when system behavior is certain and fixed.

============================================================
10. STOCHASTIC SIMULATION
============================================================

. Stochastic simulation includes randomness.

. Same input may give different output in different runs.

      Example:
      
              . Customer arrival at a bank is uncertain.
              
              . Sometimes 5 customers arrive in 10 minutes.
              
              . Sometimes 12 customers arrive in 10 minutes.
              
              . Therefore, random numbers are used.

        Use:
        
                . Suitable for uncertain systems such as:
                  . Market demand
                  . Weather
                  . Traffic
                  . Queues
                  . Stock price
                  . Patient arrival

2.2 MONTE CARLO METHOD
============================================================

------------------------------------------------------------
Meaning
------------------------------------------------------------

. Monte Carlo method is a simulation technique that uses random numbers
  and probability to solve problems.

            . It is used when the problem has uncertainty.
            
            . It performs repeated experiments using random values.
            
            . The final result is obtained by analyzing many repeated outcomes.

------------------------------------------------------------
Simple Definition
------------------------------------------------------------

. Monte Carlo simulation is a method of solving uncertain problems by
  repeatedly using random numbers and observing the possible results.

--

----------------------------------------------------------
Why It Is Called Monte Carlo
------------------------------------------------------------

. The name Monte Carlo comes from the famous casino area called Monte
  Carlo.

. Casino games depend on randomness and probability.

. Similarly, Monte Carlo simulation also uses randomness and probability.




------------------------------------------------------------
Main Idea
------------------------------------------------------------

. Instead of using only one fixed value, Monte Carlo simulation uses many
  possible random values.

. It repeats the process many times.

. Then it calculates average result, minimum result, maximum result, and
  risk.




------------------------------------------------------------
Basic Steps of Monte Carlo Simulation
------------------------------------------------------------

        . Step 1: Define the problem.
        
        . Step 2: Identify uncertain variables.
        
        . Step 3: Assign probability distribution or possible range.
        
        . Step 4: Generate random numbers.
        
        . Step 5: Calculate output for each random case.
        
        . Step 6: Repeat simulation many times.
        
        . Step 7: Analyze the result.
        
        . Step 8: Make decision.




------------------------------------------------------------
Monte Carlo Simulation Flow
------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Define problem
              |
              v
        . Identify uncertain variables
              |
              v
        . Generate random numbers
              |
              v
        . Calculate output
              |
              v
        . Repeat many times
              |
              v
        . Analyze average, risk, best case, worst case
              |
              v
        . Make decision



============================================================
MONTE CARLO EXAMPLE 1: SIMPLE PROFIT SIMULATION
============================================================

Problem:

. A small coffee business wants to estimate daily profit.

            Given:
            
                    . Selling price per cup = Rs. 120
                    
                    . Cost per cup = Rs. 70
                    
                    . Profit per cup = Rs. 120 - Rs. 70 = Rs. 50
                    
                    . Number of cups sold per day is uncertain.
            
            Possible demand:
            
                  . Low demand    = 50 cups
                  . Medium demand = 80 cups
                  . High demand   = 120 cups
            
            Probability:
                  
                  . Low demand probability    = 0.30
                  . Medium demand probability = 0.50
                  . High demand probability   = 0.20
            
            Expected cups:
            
                  . Expected cups =
                    (50 x 0.30) + (80 x 0.50) + (120 x 0.20)
                  
                            . Expected cups =  15 + 40 + 24
                            
                            . Expected cups = 79 cups
            
            Expected daily profit:
            
                  . Expected profit = 79 x Rs. 50
                  
                  . Expected profit = Rs. 3,950
            
            Conclusion:
            
                    . The coffee business may expect around Rs. 3,950 profit per day.





============================================================
MONTE CARLO EXAMPLE 2: RANDOM NUMBER TABLE FOR DEMAND
============================================================

            Demand distribution:
            
                  . 50 cups   probability 0.30
                  . 80 cups   probability 0.50
                  . 120 cups  probability 0.20
            
            Cumulative probability:
            
                    . 50 cups:
                      0.30
                    
                    . 80 cups:
                      0.30 + 0.50 = 0.80
                    
                    . 120 cups:
                      0.80 + 0.20 = 1.00
            
            Random number interval:
            
                    . 00 - 29  = 50 cups
                    
                    . 30 - 79  = 80 cups
                    
                    . 80 - 99  = 120 cups
            
            Suppose random numbers for 5 days are:
            
                    . Day 1 = 24
                    . Day 2 = 61
                    . Day 3 = 87
                    . Day 4 = 35
                    . Day 5 = 12
            
            Simulation table:
            
                    . Day 1:
                      Random number = 24
                      Demand = 50 cups
                      Profit = 50 x 50 = Rs. 2,500
                    
                    . Day 2:
                      Random number = 61
                      Demand = 80 cups
                      Profit = 80 x 50 = Rs. 4,000
                    
                    . Day 3:
                      Random number = 87
                      Demand = 120 cups
                      Profit = 120 x 50 = Rs. 6,000
                    
                    . Day 4:
                      Random number = 35
                      Demand = 80 cups
                      Profit = 80 x 50 = Rs. 4,000
                    
                    . Day 5:
                      Random number = 12
                      Demand = 50 cups
                      Profit = 50 x 50 = Rs. 2,500
            
            Total profit:
            
                    . Rs. 2,500 + Rs. 4,000 + Rs. 6,000 + Rs. 4,000 + Rs. 2,500
                    
                    . Total profit = Rs. 19,000
            
            Average daily profit:
            
                    . Rs. 19,000 / 5 = Rs. 3,800
            
            Conclusion:
            
                    . Based on 5-day simulation, average daily profit is Rs. 3,800.

2.3 COMPARISON OF SIMULATION AND ANALYTICAL METHODS
============================================================

------------------------------------------------------------
Analytical Method
------------------------------------------------------------

          . Analytical method solves a problem using mathematical formulas.
          
          . It gives exact or closed-form solutions when the system is simple.
          
          . It is based on equations, assumptions, and direct calculation.
          
          Example:
          
                      . A machine produces 10 units per hour.
                      
                      . Production in 8 hours:
                      
                        10 x 8 = 80 units
                      
                      . This is an analytical solution.



------------------------------------------------------------
Simulation Method
------------------------------------------------------------

. Simulation method solves a problem by imitating the real system.

. It uses models, random numbers, experiments, and repeated trials.

. It gives approximate results based on experiments.

            Example:
            
            . In a hospital, patient arrival is random.
            
            . Service time is also random.
            
            . Waiting time changes every day.
            
            . Simulation is used to study average waiting time.

============================================================
COMPARISON TABLE
============================================================

            . Basis:
                        Analytical Method:
                        Uses mathematical formulas.
                      
                        Simulation Method:
                        Uses imitation and experimentation.
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . Result:
                        Analytical Method:
                        Usually gives exact result.
                      
                        Simulation Method:
                        Usually gives approximate result.
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . Suitability:
                        Analytical Method:
                        Suitable for simple and well-defined systems.
                      
                        Simulation Method:
                        Suitable for complex and uncertain systems.
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . Randomness:
                        Analytical Method:
                        Difficult to handle randomness.
                      
                        Simulation Method:
                        Can easily handle randomness.
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . Cost:
                      Analytical Method:
                      Usually cheaper for simple problems.
                    
                      Simulation Method:
                      May require computer and software.
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . Time:
                      Analytical Method:
                      Faster for simple problems.
                    
                      Simulation Method:
                      May take time for model building and experiments.
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . Flexibility:
                      Analytical Method:
                      Less flexible for complex changes.
                    
                      Simulation Method:
                      Highly flexible.
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . Example:
                      Analytical Method:
                      Calculating fixed production rate.
                    
                      Simulation Method:
                      Simulating bank queue or traffic system.




============================================================
SIMPLE EXAMPLE: ANALYTICAL VS SIMULATION
============================================================

Problem:

. A ticket counter serves customers.

                Analytical approach:
                
                        . If service time is fixed at 5 minutes per customer:
                        
                          Customers served in 1 hour = 60 / 5 = 12 customers
                        
                        . This is simple and exact.
                
                Simulation approach:
                
                        . But in real life:
                          . Some customers take 2 minutes
                          . Some customers take 5 minutes
                          . Some customers take 10 minutes
                          . Customers arrive randomly
                
                . Therefore, simulation is better for finding:
                          . Average waiting time
                          . Maximum queue length
                          . Staff utilization
                          . Customer delay
                
                Conclusion:
                        
                        . Analytical method is good for simple fixed problems.
                        
                        . Simulation is better for complex, random, and real-life problems.

2.6 COMPUTATIONAL TECHNIQUES FOR CONTINUOUS MODELS
============================================================

------------------------------------------------------------
Meaning
------------------------------------------------------------

. Continuous models describe systems whose state changes continuously
  with time.

. These models are often represented using differential equations.

. Computational techniques are used to solve these equations using
  numerical methods.

------------------------------------------------------------
Continuous Model Examples
------------------------------------------------------------

          . Population growth
          
          . Water level in a tank
          
          . Temperature change
          
          . Vehicle speed
          
          . Disease spread
          
          . Chemical reaction
          
          . Economic growth
          
          . Feedback control system

------------------------------------------------------------
Why Computational Techniques Are Needed
------------------------------------------------------------

        . Many continuous models cannot be solved easily using exact
          mathematical methods.
        
        . Real systems may have complex equations.
        
        . Computer-based numerical methods help approximate the solution.
        
        . These techniques divide time into small intervals and calculate values
          step by step.

============================================================
BASIC IDEA OF NUMERICAL COMPUTATION
============================================================

. Suppose the value of a system variable changes with time.
          
          . Let the variable be y.
          
          . Let time be t.

. The rate of change is represented by dy/dt.

. A continuous model may be written as:   dy/dt = f(t, y)

. The computer calculates y at small time steps.


   
      Example:
      
      . Time step = 1 second
      
      . The computer calculates:
        . Value at 0 second
        . Value at 1 second
        . Value at 2 seconds
        . Value at 3 seconds

. In this way, continuous change is approximated.



============================================================
COMMON COMPUTATIONAL TECHNIQUES
============================================================

. 1. Euler's Method

. 2. Modified Euler Method

. 3. Runge-Kutta Method

. 4. Numerical Integration

. 5. Difference Equation Method

============================================================
1. EULER'S METHOD
============================================================

. Euler's method is a simple numerical method for solving differential
  equations.

. It estimates the next value using the current value and rate of
  change.

Formula:

. y_new = y_old + h x f(t, y)

Where:

. y_old = current value

. y_new = next value

. h = step size

. f(t, y) = rate of change

------------------------------------------------------------
Simple Example: Water Tank
------------------------------------------------------------

Problem:

. A tank contains 100 liters of water.

. Water is leaking at rate of 5 liters per hour.

. Find water level after 3 hours using step size 1 hour.

Given:

. Initial water = 100 liters

. Rate of change = -5 liters/hour

. Step size h = 1 hour

Calculation:

. After 1 hour:
  y = 100 + 1 x (-5)
  y = 95 liters

. After 2 hours:
  y = 95 + 1 x (-5)
  y = 90 liters

. After 3 hours:
  y = 90 + 1 x (-5)
  y = 85 liters

Conclusion:

. Water left after 3 hours = 85 liters

============================================================
2. MODIFIED EULER METHOD
============================================================

. Modified Euler method improves Euler's method by using average slope.

. It gives better accuracy than simple Euler method.

Basic idea:

. First estimate the next value.

. Then correct the value using average rate of change.

Use:

. When more accuracy is required than simple Euler method.

Example application:

. Temperature cooling calculation.

. Population growth approximation.

. Speed and distance calculation.

============================================================
3. RUNGE-KUTTA METHOD
============================================================

. Runge-Kutta method is a popular numerical method for solving
  differential equations.

. It gives more accurate results than Euler's method.

. The fourth-order Runge-Kutta method is commonly used.

Use:

. Engineering simulation

. Physics problems

. Control systems

. Scientific computing

. Continuous system simulation

Advantages:

. More accurate

. Stable

. Suitable for complex continuous models

Disadvantages:

. More calculation than Euler method

============================================================
4. NUMERICAL INTEGRATION
============================================================

. Numerical integration is used to approximate the area under a curve.

. It is useful when exact integration is difficult.

Common methods:

. Trapezoidal rule

. Simpson's rule

Application:

. Finding total distance from speed-time graph.

. Finding total production from production rate.

. Finding total flow from flow rate.

------------------------------------------------------------
Simple Example
------------------------------------------------------------

. If water enters a tank at 10 liters per hour for 5 hours:

  Total water = 10 x 5 = 50 liters

. If flow rate changes continuously, numerical integration is used.

============================================================
5. DIFFERENCE EQUATION METHOD
============================================================

. Difference equation method converts continuous change into stepwise
  calculations.

. It is useful in computer simulation.

Example:

. Population at next time:

  P_next = P_current + growth - death

. This is repeated for each time step.

Application:

. Population model

. Inventory model

. Economic model

. Disease spread model

============================================================
CONTINUOUS MODEL EXAMPLE: POPULATION GROWTH
============================================================

Problem:

. A town has 10,000 people.

. Population grows at 2% per year.

. Estimate population for next 3 years.

Model:

. P_next = P_current + growth

. Growth = 2% of current population

Year 0:

. Population = 10,000

Year 1:

. Growth = 10,000 x 0.02 = 200

. Population = 10,000 + 200 = 10,200

Year 2:

. Growth = 10,200 x 0.02 = 204

. Population = 10,200 + 204 = 10,404

Year 3:

. Growth = 10,404 x 0.02 = 208.08

. Population = 10,404 + 208.08 = 10,612.08

Conclusion:

. Estimated population after 3 years = approximately 10,612

============================================================
CONTINUOUS MODEL EXAMPLE: TEMPERATURE COOLING
============================================================

Problem:

. A cup of tea has temperature 90 degree Celsius.

. Room temperature is 30 degree Celsius.

. Tea cools gradually over time.

Model idea:

. Cooling rate depends on the difference between tea temperature and
  room temperature.

. Higher difference means faster cooling.

Simulation variables:

. Initial tea temperature

. Room temperature

. Cooling rate

. Time step

. Final temperature

Application:

. Heat transfer

. Food industry

. Machine cooling

. Coffee roasting temperature study















============================================================
2.7 COBWEB MODELS
============================================================

------------------------------------------------------------
Meaning
------------------------------------------------------------

. Cobweb model is a dynamic economic model that explains how price and
  quantity change over time.

. It is commonly used in markets where production decisions are based on
  previous price.

. It is called cobweb model because the graph of price and quantity
  movement may look like a cobweb.

------------------------------------------------------------
Basic Idea
------------------------------------------------------------

. Producers decide current production based on previous market price.

. But actual market price depends on current supply and demand.

. This causes price and quantity to move up and down over time.

------------------------------------------------------------
Real-Life Example
------------------------------------------------------------

. Farmers decide how much crop to produce this year based on last year's
  price.

. If last year's price was high, many farmers produce more this year.

. More production creates more supply.

. More supply may reduce price this year.

. If price becomes low this year, farmers produce less next year.

. Less production may increase price next year.

. This cycle continues.





============================================================
COBWEB MODEL IN SIMPLE STEPS
============================================================

          . Step 1:
            Last year's price is high.
          
          . Step 2:
            Producers increase production this year.
          
          . Step 3:
            Supply becomes high.
          
          . Step 4:
            Market price decreases.
          
          . Step 5:
            Producers reduce production next year.
          
          . Step 6:
            Supply becomes low.
          
          . Step 7:
            Market price increases again.
          
          . Step 8:
            The cycle repeats.





============================================================
COBWEB MODEL EXAMPLE: TOMATO MARKET
============================================================

Scenario:

      . In Nepal, suppose tomato price was high last year.
      
      . Farmers see high profit.
      
      . This year, many farmers grow tomatoes.
      
      . Market supply becomes high.
      
      . Due to excess supply, tomato price falls.
      
      . Next year, farmers reduce tomato farming.
      
      . Supply becomes low.
      
      . Tomato price rises again.

Conclusion:
        
        . The price and quantity move in a cycle.
        
        . Cobweb model helps explain this type of market fluctuation.

============================================================
COBWEB MODEL EXAMPLE: COFFEE FARMING
============================================================

Scenario:

        . Suppose parchment coffee price is high this year.
        
        . Farmers become interested in coffee farming.
        
        . They plant more coffee or collect more coffee.
        
        . After some time, supply increases.
        
        . If demand does not increase equally, price may fall.
        
        . When price falls, some farmers may reduce coffee production.
        
        . Later, supply decreases and price may rise again.

Conclusion:

        . Coffee price and supply may fluctuate over time.
        
        . Cobweb model helps understand delayed response between price and
          production.



============================================================
MATHEMATICAL IDEA OF COBWEB MODEL
============================================================

. Demand function:

  Qd = a - bP

. Supply function:

  Qs = c + dP_previous

Where:

. Qd = quantity demanded

. Qs = quantity supplied

. P = current price

. P_previous = previous period price

. a, b, c, d = constants

Basic logic:

. Current supply depends on previous price.

. Current price is determined by current demand and supply.

============================================================
SIMPLE NUMERICAL EXAMPLE OF COBWEB MODEL
============================================================

Demand function:

. Qd = 100 - 2P

Supply function:

. Qs = 20 + 2P_previous

              Suppose previous price:
              
              . P_previous = 20
              
              Step 1: Calculate current supply
              
              . Qs = 20 + 2 x 20
              
              . Qs = 20 + 40
              
              . Qs = 60 units
              
              Step 2: Market equilibrium condition
              
              . Qd = Qs
              
              . 100 - 2P = 60
              
              . 2P = 40
              
              . P = 20
              
              Current price:
              
              . P = 20

Conclusion:

. If previous price is 20, current supply is 60 and current price is
  also 20.




------------------------------------------------------------
Another Case
------------------------------------------------------------

Suppose previous price:

. P_previous = 30

              Step 1: Calculate current supply
              
              . Qs = 20 + 2 x 30
              
              . Qs = 80 units
              
              Step 2: Calculate current price
              
              . Qd = Qs
              
              . 100 - 2P = 80
              
              . 2P = 20
              
              . P = 10

Conclusion:

. If previous price is high, producers supply more.

. More supply causes current price to fall.



============================================================
TYPES OF COBWEB BEHAVIOR
============================================================

. 1. Convergent Cobweb

. 2. Divergent Cobweb

. 3. Continuous / Stable Cobweb

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Convergent Cobweb
------------------------------------------------------------

          . Price and quantity fluctuations become smaller over time.
          
          . The system gradually moves toward equilibrium.
          
          Example:
          
          . Tomato price fluctuates but slowly becomes stable.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Divergent Cobweb
------------------------------------------------------------

        . Price and quantity fluctuations become larger over time.
        
        . The system moves away from equilibrium.
        
        Example:
        
        . Price rises very high one year and crashes very low next year.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Continuous / Stable Cobweb
------------------------------------------------------------

        . Price and quantity continue to fluctuate in a regular cycle.
        
        . The system neither settles nor explodes.
        
        Example:
        
        . Seasonal agricultural prices moving in repeated cycles.

============================================================
IMPORTANCE OF COBWEB MODEL
============================================================

            . It explains price fluctuation in markets.
            
            . It helps understand delayed production response.
            
            . It is useful in agricultural economics.
            
            . It helps in forecasting supply and demand.
            
            . It helps in policy planning.
            
            . It supports decision making in production and pricing.
            
            . It shows how unstable markets can arise from delayed decisions.

============================================================
LIMITATIONS OF COBWEB MODEL
============================================================
          
          . It assumes producers depend only on previous price.
          
          . It may not include all real market factors.
          
          . It may ignore government policies.
          
          . It may ignore storage and import/export.
          
          . It may ignore weather and production risk.
          
          . It may not fit all markets.
          
          . It gives simplified explanation of real-world price movement.
