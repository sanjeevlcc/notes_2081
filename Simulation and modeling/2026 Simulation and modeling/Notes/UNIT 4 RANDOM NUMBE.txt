      4.1 Random Number Properties and Tables
      ============================================================
      
      . What is Random Number?
      
        . Random number means a number selected without bias.
        . Every value should have equal chance of selection.
      
        Example:
        Teacher wants to randomly select students for weight analysis.
      
        Student Numbers:
        01, 02, 03, 04, 05, ..., 15
      
        Random selection:
        03, 12, 08, 15
      
        Selected Students:
        . 03 = Gita
        . 12 = Anu
        . 08 = Bina
        . 15 = Pooja
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Properties of Random Numbers
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      1. Uniformity
      
        . Each student must have equal chance of selection.
        . No student should have higher priority.
      
        Example:
        . Ram = equal chance
        . Gita = equal chance
        . Pooja = equal chance
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      2. Independence
      
        . Selection of one student should not affect another.
      
        Example:
        . Selecting Ram should not affect selecting Shyam.
      
      
      
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Random Number Table
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Before computers, random number tables were used.
      
      Example Table:
      
      Row 1 : 031208
      Row 2 : 151104
      Row 3 : 060913
      
      Read two digits at a time:
      
      03 → Gita
      12 → Anu
      08 → Bina
      15 → Pooja
      
      
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Short Note
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Random numbers are used for random selection.
      . Good random numbers must have:
        . Uniformity
        . Independence
      . Random number tables were used before computers.

4.2 Pseudo-Random Numbers
============================================================
      
      . What is Pseudo-Random Number?
      
        . Pseudo-random numbers are generated by computer algorithms.
        . They look random but follow mathematical formulas.
      
      Example:
      0.23, 0.67, 0.45, 0.91
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Example Using Student Weights
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Suppose computer generates:
      
      0.12
      0.80
      0.35
      0.53
      0.93
      
      Formula:
      
      Student Number = Random Number × 15
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      1) Random Number = 0.12
      
      0.12 × 15 = 1.8 ≈ 2
      
      Selected Student:
      02 = Shyam
      
      Weight:
      44 kg
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      2) Random Number = 0.80
      
      0.80 × 15 = 12
      
      Selected Student:
      12 = Anu
      
      Weight:
      46 kg
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      3) Random Number = 0.35
      
      0.35 × 15 = 5.25 ≈ 5
      
      Selected Student:
      05 = Rita
      
      Weight:
      52 kg
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      4) Random Number = 0.53
      
      0.53 × 15 = 7.95 ≈ 8
      
      Selected Student:
      08 = Bina
      
      Weight:
      55 kg
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      5) Random Number = 0.93
      
      0.93 × 15 = 13.95 ≈ 14
      
      Selected Student:
      14 = Roshan
      
      Weight:
      67 kg
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Selected Students Summary
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Shyam   = 44 kg
      Anu     = 46 kg
      Rita    = 52 kg
      Bina    = 55 kg
      Roshan  = 67 kg
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Average Weight
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Average Weight =
      (44 + 46 + 52 + 55 + 67) / 5
      
      = 264 / 5
      = 52.8 kg
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Advantages of Pseudo-Random Numbers
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Fast generation
      . Easy to generate
      . Useful in simulation
      . Computer friendly
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Disadvantages of Pseudo-Random Numbers
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Not truly random
      . Can repeat after some cycles

============================================================
4.3 RANDOM NUMBER GENERATION METHODS
============================================================

. Random number generation means producing random numbers for simulation.

. In computer simulation, random numbers are usually generated by formulas.

. These generated numbers are called pseudo-random numbers.

. Two common methods are:

  . Mid-square method
  . Residue method


============================================================
A. MID-SQUARE METHOD
============================================================

. The mid-square method is a simple random number generation method.

. It was proposed by John von Neumann.

. In this method:

  . First, choose a seed number.
  . Square the seed number.
  . Take the middle digits from the squared value.
  . Use those middle digits as the next random number.
  . Repeat the process.


------------------------------------------------------------
Steps of Mid-Square Method
------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Step 1: Choose a seed number.
      
      . Step 2: Square the seed number.
      
      . Step 3: If needed, add zeros to make fixed number of digits.
      
      . Step 4: Select middle digits.
      
      . Step 5: Use selected middle digits as next seed.
      
      . Step 6: Repeat the process.

          
          ------------------------------------------------------------
          Example 1: Mid-Square Method
          ------------------------------------------------------------
          
          . Let seed number be:
          
            X0 = 5735
          
          . Since seed has 4 digits, we take 4 middle digits each time.




        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Iteration 1
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Seed:
        
          X0 = 5735
        
        . Square the seed:
        
          5735 × 5735 = 32890225
        
        . Middle 4 digits:
        
          8902
        
        . Therefore:
        
          X1 = 8902
        
        . Random number:
        
          R1 = 0.8902
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Iteration 2
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . New seed:
        
          X1 = 8902
        
        . Square the seed:
        
          8902 × 8902 = 79245604
        
        . Middle 4 digits:
        
          2456
        
        . Therefore:
        
          X2 = 2456
        
        . Random number:
        
          R2 = 0.2456
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Iteration 3
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . New seed:
        
          X2 = 2456
        
        . Square the seed:
        
          2456 × 2456 = 06031936
        
        . Middle 4 digits:
        
          0319
        
        . Therefore:
        
          X3 = 0319
        
        . Random number:
        
          R3 = 0.0319
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Iteration 4
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . New seed:
        
          X3 = 0319
        
        . Square the seed:
        
          0319 × 0319 = 000101761
        
        . Make it 8 digits if needed:
        
          0101761 can be written as 00101761
        
        . Middle 4 digits:
        
          1017
        
        . Therefore:
        
          X4 = 1017
        
        . Random number:
        
          R4 = 0.1017
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Mid-Square Result Table
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Seed length = 4 digits
        
        +-------------+----------+------------+----------------+---------------+
        | Iteration   | Seed     | Square     | Middle 4 Digits| Random Number |
        +-------------+----------+------------+----------------+---------------+
        | 1           | 5735     | 32890225   | 8902           | 0.8902        |
        | 2           | 8902     | 79245604   | 2456           | 0.2456        |
        | 3           | 2456     | 06031936   | 0319           | 0.0319        |
        | 4           | 0319     | 00101761   | 1017           | 0.1017        |
        +-------------+----------+------------+----------------+---------------+

------------------------------------------------------------
Advantages of Mid-Square Method
------------------------------------------------------------

. Easy to understand.

. Simple calculation.

. Useful for teaching random number generation.

. Does not require many parameters.


------------------------------------------------------------
Disadvantages of Mid-Square Method
------------------------------------------------------------

. Poor randomness.

. Sequence may repeat quickly.

. Seed may become zero.

. Not suitable for large practical simulation.

. Quality depends heavily on seed value.






============================================================
B. RESIDUE METHOD
============================================================

. Residue method is also called the Linear Congruential Method.

. It is one of the most common methods for generating pseudo-random numbers.

. It uses modulus operation.

. The next number is generated from the previous number.

        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Formula of Residue Method
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Formula:
        
          X(n+1) = (aXn + c) mod m
        
                . Where:
                
                      Xn = current seed value
                    
                      X(n+1) = next generated value
                    
                            a = multiplier
                          
                            c = increment
                          
                            m = modulus
                          
                            mod = remainder after division
    

------------------------------------------------------------
Steps of Residue Method
------------------------------------------------------------

      . Step 1: Choose values of a, c, m, and seed X0.
      
      . Step 2: Use formula:
      
        X(n+1) = (aXn + c) mod m
      
      . Step 3: Divide generated value by m to get random number between 0 and 1.
      
        Rn = Xn / m
      
      . Step 4: Repeat the process.




------------------------------------------------------------
Example 2: Residue Method
------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Given:
      
        a = 5
        c = 3
        m = 16
        X0 = 7
      
      . Formula:
      
        X(n+1) = (5Xn + 3) mod 16


------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1
------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X0 = 7
            
            . X1 = (5 × 7 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X1 = (35 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X1 = 38 mod 16
            
            . X1 = 6
            
            . Random number:
            
              R1 = X1 / m
            
              R1 = 6 / 16
            
              R1 = 0.375
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Iteration 2
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X1 = 6
            
            . X2 = (5 × 6 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X2 = (30 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X2 = 33 mod 16
            
            . X2 = 1
            
            . Random number:
            
              R2 = 1 / 16
            
              R2 = 0.0625
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Iteration 3
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X2 = 1
            
            . X3 = (5 × 1 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X3 = 8 mod 16
            
            . X3 = 8
            
            . Random number:
            
              R3 = 8 / 16
            
              R3 = 0.5
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Iteration 4
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X3 = 8
            
            . X4 = (5 × 8 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X4 = 43 mod 16
            
            . X4 = 11
            
            . Random number:
            
              R4 = 11 / 16
            
              R4 = 0.6875
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Iteration 5
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X4 = 11
            
            . X5 = (5 × 11 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X5 = 58 mod 16
            
            . X5 = 10
            
            . Random number:
            
              R5 = 10 / 16
            
              R5 = 0.625
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Residue Method Result Table
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            +-------------+------+---------------------------+------+---------------+
            | Iteration   | Xn   | Formula                   | Xn+1 | Random Number |
            +-------------+------+---------------------------+------+---------------+
            | 1           | 7    | (5×7 + 3) mod 16          | 6    | 0.3750        |
            | 2           | 6    | (5×6 + 3) mod 16          | 1    | 0.0625        |
            | 3           | 1    | (5×1 + 3) mod 16          | 8    | 0.5000        |
            | 4           | 8    | (5×8 + 3) mod 16          | 11   | 0.6875        |
            | 5           | 11   | (5×11 + 3) mod 16         | 10   | 0.6250        |
            +-------------+------+---------------------------+------+---------------+

      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Advantages of Residue Method
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Easy to implement in computer.
      
      . Faster than manual random number tables.
      
      . Better than mid-square method.
      
      . Widely used in simulation.
      
      . Can generate long sequences if parameters are selected properly.
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Disadvantages of Residue Method
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Generated numbers are not truly random.
      
      . Numbers may repeat after some cycle.
      
      . Poor parameter selection gives poor randomness.
      
      . Needs testing before using in simulation.
      










============================================================
Comparison Between Mid-Square and Residue Method
============================================================

+----------------------+-----------------------------+-----------------------------+
| Basis                | Mid-Square Method           | Residue Method              |
+----------------------+-----------------------------+-----------------------------+
| Main idea            | Square seed and take middle | Use modulus formula         |
| Formula              | Seed², take middle digits   | X(n+1)=(aXn+c) mod m        |
| Parameters needed    | Seed only                   | a, c, m, and seed           |
| Speed                | Simple but less reliable    | Fast and efficient          |
| Randomness quality   | Poor                        | Better if parameters good   |
| Repetition problem   | High chance                 | Depends on parameters       |
| Practical use        | Mostly teaching purpose     | More practical              |
+----------------------+-----------------------------+-----------------------------+
