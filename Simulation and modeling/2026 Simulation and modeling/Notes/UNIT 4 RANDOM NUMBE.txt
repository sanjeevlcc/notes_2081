      4.1 Random Number Properties and Tables
      ============================================================
      
      . What is Random Number?
      
        . Random number means a number selected without bias.
        . Every value should have equal chance of selection.
      
        Example:
        Teacher wants to randomly select students for weight analysis.
      
        Student Numbers:
        01, 02, 03, 04, 05, ..., 15
      
        Random selection:
        03, 12, 08, 15
      
        Selected Students:
        . 03 = Gita
        . 12 = Anu
        . 08 = Bina
        . 15 = Pooja
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Properties of Random Numbers
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      1. Uniformity
      
        . Each student must have equal chance of selection.
        . No student should have higher priority.
      
        Example:
        . Ram = equal chance
        . Gita = equal chance
        . Pooja = equal chance
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      2. Independence
      
        . Selection of one student should not affect another.
      
        Example:
        . Selecting Ram should not affect selecting Shyam.
      
      
      
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Random Number Table
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Before computers, random number tables were used.
      
      Example Table:
      
      Row 1 : 031208
      Row 2 : 151104
      Row 3 : 060913
      
      Read two digits at a time:
      
      03 → Gita
      12 → Anu
      08 → Bina
      15 → Pooja
      
      
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Short Note
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Random numbers are used for random selection.
      . Good random numbers must have:
        . Uniformity
        . Independence

============================================================
4.3 RANDOM NUMBER GENERATION METHODS
============================================================

. Random number generation means producing random numbers for simulation.

. In computer simulation, random numbers are usually generated by formulas.

. These generated numbers are called pseudo-random numbers.

. Two common methods are:

  . Mid-square method
  . Residue method


============================================================
A. MID-SQUARE METHOD
============================================================

. The mid-square method is a simple random number generation method.

. It was proposed by John von Neumann.

. In this method:

  . First, choose a seed number.
  . Square the seed number.
  . Take the middle digits from the squared value.
  . Use those middle digits as the next random number.
  . Repeat the process.


------------------------------------------------------------
Steps of Mid-Square Method
------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Step 1: Choose a seed number.
      
      . Step 2: Square the seed number.
      
      . Step 3: If needed, add zeros to make fixed number of digits.
      
      . Step 4: Select middle digits.
      
      . Step 5: Use selected middle digits as next seed.
      
      . Step 6: Repeat the process.

          
          ------------------------------------------------------------
          Example 1: Mid-Square Method
          ------------------------------------------------------------
          
          . Let seed number be:
          
            X0 = 5735
          
          . Since seed has 4 digits, we take 4 middle digits each time.




        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Iteration 1
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Seed:
        
          X0 = 5735
        
        . Square the seed:
        
          5735 × 5735 = 32890225
        
        . Middle 4 digits:
        
          8902
        
        . Therefore:
        
          X1 = 8902
        
        . Random number:
        
          R1 = 0.8902
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Iteration 2
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . New seed:
        
          X1 = 8902
        
        . Square the seed:
        
          8902 × 8902 = 79245604
        
        . Middle 4 digits:
        
          2456
        
        . Therefore:
        
          X2 = 2456
        
        . Random number:
        
          R2 = 0.2456
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Iteration 3
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . New seed:
        
          X2 = 2456
        
        . Square the seed:
        
          2456 × 2456 = 06031936
        
        . Middle 4 digits:
        
          0319
        
        . Therefore:
        
          X3 = 0319
        
        . Random number:
        
          R3 = 0.0319
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Iteration 4
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . New seed:
        
          X3 = 0319
        
        . Square the seed:
        
          0319 × 0319 = 000101761
        
        . Make it 8 digits if needed:
        
          0101761 can be written as 00101761
        
        . Middle 4 digits:
        
          1017
        
        . Therefore:
        
          X4 = 1017
        
        . Random number:
        
          R4 = 0.1017
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Mid-Square Result Table
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Seed length = 4 digits

        | Iteration   | Seed     | Square     | Middle 4 Digits| Random Number |

============================================================
B. RESIDUE METHOD
============================================================

. Residue method is also called the Linear Congruential Method.

. It is one of the most common methods for generating pseudo-random numbers.

. It uses modulus operation.

. The next number is generated from the previous number.

        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        Formula of Residue Method
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Formula:
        
          X(n+1) = (aXn + c) mod m
        
                . Where:
                
                      Xn = current seed value
                    
                      X(n+1) = next generated value
                    
                            a = multiplier
                          
                            c = increment
                          
                            m = modulus
                          
                            mod = remainder after division
    

------------------------------------------------------------
Steps of Residue Method
------------------------------------------------------------

      . Step 1: Choose values of a, c, m, and seed X0.
      
      . Step 2: Use formula:
      
        X(n+1) = (aXn + c) mod m
      
      . Step 3: Divide generated value by m to get random number between 0 and 1.
      
        Rn = Xn / m
      
      . Step 4: Repeat the process.




------------------------------------------------------------
Example 2: Residue Method
------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Given:
      
        a = 5
        c = 3
        m = 16
        X0 = 7
      
      . Formula:
      
        X(n+1) = (5Xn + 3) mod 16


------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1
------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X0 = 7
            
            . X1 = (5 × 7 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X1 = (35 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X1 = 38 mod 16
            
            . X1 = 6
            
            . Random number:
            
              R1 = X1 / m
            
              R1 = 6 / 16
            
              R1 = 0.375
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Iteration 2
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X1 = 6
            
            . X2 = (5 × 6 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X2 = (30 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X2 = 33 mod 16
            
            . X2 = 1
            
            . Random number:
            
              R2 = 1 / 16
            
              R2 = 0.0625
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Iteration 3
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X2 = 1
            
            . X3 = (5 × 1 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X3 = 8 mod 16
            
            . X3 = 8
            
            . Random number:
            
              R3 = 8 / 16
            
              R3 = 0.5
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Iteration 4
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X3 = 8
            
            . X4 = (5 × 8 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X4 = 43 mod 16
            
            . X4 = 11
            
            . Random number:
            
              R4 = 11 / 16
            
              R4 = 0.6875
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Iteration 5
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            . X4 = 11
            
            . X5 = (5 × 11 + 3) mod 16
            
            . X5 = 58 mod 16
            
            . X5 = 10
            
            . Random number:
            
              R5 = 10 / 16
            
              R5 = 0.625
            
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            Residue Method Result Table
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            +-------------+------+---------------------------+------+---------------+
            | Iteration   | Xn   | Formula                   | Xn+1 | Random Number |
            +-------------+------+---------------------------+------+---------------+

4.4 TESTING NUMBERS FOR RANDOMNESS

. After generating random numbers, we must test whether they are random or not.

. Good random numbers should satisfy:
  . Uniformity
  . Independence

. Two important tests are:
  . Uniformity Test
  . Chi-Square Test


============================================================
A. UNIFORMITY TEST
============================================================

. Theory:

  . Uniformity test checks whether random numbers are equally distributed
    between 0 and 1.

  . If numbers are equally distributed in all intervals, they pass the test.


------------------------------------------------------------
Example
------------------------------------------------------------

Generated random numbers:

0.12, 0.18, 0.26, 0.34, 0.47,
0.51, 0.63, 0.72, 0.89, 0.95

Divide into intervals:

(0,0.2)
(0.2,0.4)
(0.4,0.6)
(0.6,0.8)
(0.8,1.0)

------------------------------------------------------------
Observed Frequency
------------------------------------------------------------

(0,0.2)   = 2
(0.2,0.4) = 2
(0.4,0.6) = 2
(0.6,0.8) = 2
(0.8,1.0) = 2

------------------------------------------------------------
Conclusion
------------------------------------------------------------

. All intervals have equal frequency.

. Therefore random numbers are uniformly distributed.


============================================================
B. CHI-SQUARE TEST
============================================================

. Theory:

  . Chi-Square test is used to statistically test uniformity.

  . It compares:
    . Observed Frequency (Oi)
    . Expected Frequency (Ei)


------------------------------------------------------------
Formula
------------------------------------------------------------

χ² = Σ ((Oi − Ei)² / Ei)

Where:

Oi = Observed frequency
Ei = Expected frequency




The initial 100 random numbers
      43, 09, 52, 98,
      78, 44, 21, 12,
      38, 67, 97, 46, 07, 18, 49, 77,
      67, 77, 37, 76, 65, 14, 25, 37,
      74, 03, 71, 28, 65, 05, 54, 13, 97,
      17, 32, 91, 28, 59, 56, 73,
      99, 71, 99, 64, 5, 66, 01, 24, 73,
      15, 45, 10, 18, 82, 96, 43, 27, 34,
      65, 79, 03, 49, 69, 15, 6, 93, 48,
      42, 04, 46, 04, 11, 81, 62, 79,
      85, 49, 74, 06, 11, 64, 76, 22, 63,
      99, 2, 83, 5, 93, 24, 81, 94, 53,
      91, 37, 5, 97, 26, 92, 67


============================================================
Algorithm of Chi-Square Test
============================================================

Step 1: Define Hypothesis

H₀ : Numbers are uniformly distributed
H₁ : Numbers are not uniformly distributed


------------------------------------------------------------
Step 2: Divide into Equal Intervals
------------------------------------------------------------

From board:

N = 100 random numbers
n = 10 intervals

Intervals:

(0,0.1)
(0.1,0.2)
(0.2,0.3)
(0.3,0.4)
(0.4,0.5)
(0.5,0.6)
(0.6,0.7)
(0.7,0.8)
(0.8,0.9)
(0.9,1.0)


------------------------------------------------------------
Step 3: Expected Frequency
------------------------------------------------------------

Formula:

Ei = N / n

Calculation:

Ei = 100 / 10
Ei = 10

Therefore:

Expected frequency for each interval = 10


------------------------------------------------------------
Step 4: Observed Frequency (Board Data)
------------------------------------------------------------

Interval      Oi

(0,0.1)       8
(0.1,0.2)     9
(0.2,0.3)     10
(0.3,0.4)     13
(0.4,0.5)     8
(0.5,0.6)     8
(0.6,0.7)     11
(0.7,0.8)     7
(0.8,0.9)     9
(0.9,1.0)     16

Total = 100


------------------------------------------------------------
Step 5: Compute Chi-Square Value
------------------------------------------------------------

Interval      Oi    Ei    (Oi−Ei)²/Ei

(0,0.1)       8     10      0.4
(0.1,0.2)     9     10      0.1
(0.2,0.3)     10    10      0
(0.3,0.4)     13    10      0.9
(0.4,0.5)     8     10      0.4
(0.5,0.6)     8     10      0.4
(0.6,0.7)     11    10      0.1
(0.7,0.8)     7     10      0.9
(0.8,0.9)     9     10      0.1
(0.9,1.0)     16    10      3.6


------------------------------------------------------------
Total Chi-Square
------------------------------------------------------------

χ² = 0.4 + 0.1 + 0 + 0.9 + 0.4 + 0.4 + 0.1 + 0.9 + 0.1 + 3.6

χ² = 6.9


------------------------------------------------------------
Step 6: Critical Value
------------------------------------------------------------

Degree of Freedom:

df = n − 1

df = 10 − 1
df = 9

At α = 0.05

From chi-square table:

χ²(0.05,9) = 16.919


------------------------------------------------------------
Step 7: Decision
------------------------------------------------------------

Calculated value = 6.9
Critical value   = 16.919

Since:

6.9 < 16.919

Therefore:

Accept H₀


------------------------------------------------------------
Final Conclusion
------------------------------------------------------------

. Random numbers follow uniform distribution.

. There is no significant difference between observed and expected values.

. Therefore generated random numbers are random.




============================================================
Final Summary
============================================================
            
            Uniformity Test
            
            . Checks whether random numbers are equally distributed.
            
            ------------------------------------------------------------
            
            Chi-Square Test
            
            . Statistical test used to verify uniformity.
            
            Formula:
            
            χ² = Σ ((Oi − Ei)² / Ei)
            
            Decision Rule:
            
            If χ² calculated < χ² critical → Accept H₀
            If χ² calculated > χ² critical → Reject H₀

            46–51         3

            58–63         3

            Check total:

            Correct.
      
      ============================================================
      Step 5: Compute Chi-Square Value
      ============================================================
      
      Formula:
      
      χ² = Σ ((Oi − Ei)² / Ei)
      
      Calculation Table:
      
            Interval      Oi    Ei    (Oi−Ei)²/Ei

      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Total Chi-Square

============================================================
Autocorrelation Test
(Algorithm + Solved Example)
============================================================


4.5 TESTING FOR AUTOCORRELATION

. Autocorrelation test checks whether random numbers are independent.

. It tests whether one random number depends on another.

. Good random numbers must be:
  . Uniform
  . Independent

. If dependency exists → autocorrelation exists.


Example:

      Bad sequence:
      10, 20, 30, 40, 50
      
            . Pattern exists
            . Numbers are dependent
            . Not random
      
      
      Good sequence:
      16, 82, 30, 04, 63
      
            . No obvious pattern
            . More random
      

============================================================
AUTOCORRELATION TEST ALGORITHM
============================================================

      Step 1: Define Hypothesis
      
      H₀ : Random numbers are independent
      
      H₁ : Random numbers are dependent
      
      
      Mathematically:
      
      H₀ : Ŝim = 0
      H₁ : Ŝim ≠ 0
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Step 2: Find i and lag m
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      i = starting position
      m = lag
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Step 3: Find M
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Using:
      
      i + (M+1)m ≤ N
      
      Where:
      
      N = total number of random numbers
      M = largest integer satisfying condition
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Step 4: Compute Estimator
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Formula:
      
      Ŝim = [1/(M+1)] Σ(Ri+km × Ri+(k+1)m) − 0.25
      
      where k = 0 to M
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Step 5: Compute Standard Deviation
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Formula:
      
      σ(Ŝim) = √(13M+7) / [12(M+1)]
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Step 6: Compute Test Statistic
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      Formula:
      
      Z₀ = Ŝim / σ(Ŝim)
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Step 7: Find Critical Value
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      At α = 0.05
      
      Z₀.₀₂₅ = 1.96
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      Step 8: Decision
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      If:
      
      −1.96 ≤ Z₀ ≤ +1.96
      
      Accept H₀
      
      → Numbers are independent
      
      
      Else:
      
      Reject H₀
      
      → Numbers are dependent





============================================================
 EXAMPLE 
============================================================

      Given random numbers:
      
      0.19, 0.16, 0.82,
      0.63, 0.04, 0.16,
      0.30, 0.22, 0.85, 0.48, 0.27, 0.55,
      0.44, 0.05, 0.81, 0.35, 0.59, 0.33, 0.61,
      0.73, 0.48, 0.75, 0.57, 0.19, 0.20, 0.14,
      0.46, 0.58, 0.73, 0.15, 0.02, 0.49, 0.36,
      0.24, 0.90, 0.74, 0.41, 0.04, 0.29
      
      
      Total numbers:
      
      N = 40
      
      
      ============================================================
      Step 1: Define Hypothesis
      ============================================================
      
      H₀ : Numbers are independent
      
      H₁ : Numbers are dependent
      
      
      ============================================================
      Step 2: Choose i and m
      ============================================================
      
      Given:
      
      i = 2
      m = 5
      
      
      ============================================================
      Step 3: Find M
      ============================================================
      
      Formula:
      
      i + (M+1)m ≤ N
      
      Substitute values:
      
      2 + (M+1)(5) ≤ 40
      
      2 + 5M + 5 ≤ 40
      
      7 + 5M ≤ 40
      
      5M ≤ 33
      
      M ≤ 6.6
      
      Largest integer:
      
      M = 6
      
      
      ============================================================
      Step 4: Compute Estimator
      ============================================================
      
      Formula:
      
      Ŝim = [1/(M+1)] Σ(Ri+km × Ri+(k+1)m) − 0.25
      
      Substituting M = 6:
      
      Ŝim = 1/7 [Σ products] − 0.25
      
      
      From board:
      
      Ŝim = −0.0193
      
      
      ============================================================
      Step 5: Compute Standard Deviation
      ============================================================
      
      Formula:
      
      σ(Ŝim) = √(13M+7) / [12(M+1)]
      
      Substitute M = 6:
      
      σ(Ŝim) = √(13×6 + 7) / [12(6+1)]
      
      = √85 / 84
      
      = 0.10975
      
      
      ============================================================
      Step 6: Compute Z Statistic
      ============================================================
      
      Formula:
      
      Z₀ = Ŝim / σ(Ŝim)
      
      Z₀ = −0.0193 / 0.10975
      
      Z₀ = −0.17
      
      
      ============================================================
      Step 7: Critical Value
      ============================================================
      
      At α = 0.05
      
      Critical value:
      
      Z₀.₀₂₅ = 1.96
      
      
      Acceptance region:
      
      −1.96 ≤ Z₀ ≤ +1.96
      
      
      ============================================================
      Step 8: Decision
      ============================================================
      
      Calculated value:
      
      Z₀ = −0.17
      
      Compare:
      
      −1.96 ≤ −0.17 ≤ +1.96
      
      
      Condition satisfied.
      
      Accept H₀
      
      
      ============================================================
      Final Conclusion
      ============================================================
      
      . Random numbers are independent.
      
      . No significant autocorrelation exists.
      
      . Therefore numbers pass autocorrelation test.
