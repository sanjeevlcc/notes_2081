CASE SCENARIO:
PHYSICAL UNIVERSITY FORM REGISTRATION COUNTER
(Pokhara University Admission Form Submission)
============================================================

  . Assume Pokhara University opens physical form registration.
  
  . Students arrive to submit forms physically.
  
  . There are limited counters for service.
  
  . Students may need to wait in line.
  
  . This creates a queue (waiting line).





Example:

      At 10:00 AM:
          . 20 students arrive in 15 minutes.
          . Only 2 counters are available.
          . Each counter takes around 3 minutes per student.
      
      This creates congestion and waiting.
      
      
      Real-Life Examples of Queue:
          . Bank counter
          . Hospital registration
          . University form submission
          . ATM queue
          . Supermarket billing





============================================================
5.1 ELEMENTS OF QUEUING SYSTEM
============================================================

. A queuing system has several important elements.

Main Elements:

    . Calling Population
    . Arrival Process
    . Queue
    . Queue Discipline
    . Service Mechanism
    . Departure Process


        ------------------------------------------------------------
        1. Calling Population
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Calling population means source of customers.
        
        In this scenario:
        
        Calling population = Students coming for registration
        
        Example:
        
        Total students expected = 500
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        2. Arrival Process
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Arrival process means how customers arrive.
        
        Students may arrive:
        . One by one
        . In groups
        . Randomly
        
        Example:
        
        Arrival rate = λ (lambda)
        
        Suppose:
        
        60 students arrive per hour
        
        Then:
        
        λ = 60 students/hour
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        3. Queue (Waiting Line)
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Queue means waiting line.
        
        Example:
        
        10 students waiting outside registration counter.
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        4. Queue Discipline
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Queue discipline means service rule.
        
        Common types:
        
        . FIFO (First In First Out)
        . LIFO (Last In First Out)
        . Priority-based
        
        In university registration:
        
        Usually FIFO is used.
        
        Example:
        
        Student who comes first gets served first.
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        5. Service Mechanism
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . Service mechanism defines how service is provided.
        
        Includes:
        
        . Number of servers
        . Service rate
        . Service time
        
        Example:
        
        2 counters available
        
        Each counter serves 20 students/hour
        
        Service rate = μ (mu)
        
        μ = 20 students/hour per counter
        
        
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        6. Departure Process
        ------------------------------------------------------------
        
        . After service, customer leaves system.
        
        Example:
        
        After form submission, student exits queue.
        
        
        
        
        
        
        
        ============================================================
        Example Flow of Queue
        ============================================================
        
                Student Arrives
                      ↓
                Waits in Queue
                      ↓
                Gets Service at Counter
                      ↓
                Leaves System

5.2 CHARACTERISTICS OF QUEUING SYSTEM
============================================================

. Characteristics describe queue behavior.

Important characteristics:
      
      . Arrival Pattern
      . Service Pattern
      . Queue Length
      . Waiting Time
      . System Capacity
      . Number of Servers
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      1. Arrival Pattern
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Describes how customers arrive.
      
      Example:
      
      During lunch break many students arrive.
      
      Morning:
      Low arrival
      
      Afternoon:
      High arrival
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      2. Service Pattern
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Describes how service happens.
      
      Example:
      
      Some students submit quickly.
      Some take more time.
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      3. Queue Length
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Number of customers waiting.
      
      Example:
      
      Queue length = 15 students
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      4. Waiting Time
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Time customer waits before service.
      
      Example:
      
      Student waits 10 minutes before reaching counter.
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      5. System Capacity
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Maximum customers system can hold.
      
      Example:
      
      Waiting hall capacity = 50 students
      
      
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      6. Number of Servers
      ------------------------------------------------------------
      
      . Number of service counters.
      
      Example:
      
      2 counters





============================================================
Mathematical Understanding
============================================================

Given:

      Arrival rate:
      λ = 60 students/hour
      
      Service rate:
      μ = 20 students/hour per counter
      
      Number of servers:
      s = 2
      
      
      Total service capacity:
      
      s × μ
      
      = 2 × 20
      = 40 students/hour
      
      
      Compare:
      
      Arrival Rate = 60 students/hour
      Service Capacity = 40 students/hour
      
      
      Observation:
      
      60 > 40
      
      This means:
      
      . Students arrive faster than service capacity.
      . Queue grows longer.
      . Waiting time increases.






------------------------------------------------------------
Case 2
------------------------------------------------------------
    
    Suppose 4 counters are opened.
    
    Service capacity:
    
    4 × 20 = 80 students/hour
    
    Compare:
    
    Arrival = 60
    Service = 80
    
    
    Observation:
    
    60 < 80
    
    Now system becomes stable.

5.3 MODEL OF QUEUING SYSTEM
============================================================

. Queue model mathematically represents queuing system.

      A queue model helps analyze:
      
            . Waiting time
            . Queue length
            . Server utilization
            . System performance
            

General Queue Model:

      Input → Queue → Service → Output


Example:

Students Arrive → Wait → Registration Counter → Exit




============================================================
Common Queue Models
============================================================

. Single Server Queue
. Multiple Server Queue


------------------------------------------------------------
1. Single Server Queue
------------------------------------------------------------

Only one counter.

Example:

One registration officer.

Flow:

Students → Queue → Counter → Exit


Problem:

. Long waiting time
. Large queue


------------------------------------------------------------
2. Multi Server Queue
------------------------------------------------------------

Multiple counters.

Example:

4 registration counters.

Flow:

Students → Queue → Multiple Counters → Exit


Advantages:

. Faster service
. Less waiting time


============================================================
Queuing Notation Example
============================================================

Simple model:

M/M/1

Meaning:

First M = Markov arrival
Second M = Markov service
1 = One server


Example:

One bank counter


Another model:

M/M/3

Meaning:

3 service counters


Example:

University registration with 3 counters


============================================================
Numerical Example
============================================================

Case:

Arrival rate:
λ = 15 students/hour

Service rate:
μ = 20 students/hour

Single server model:
M/M/1


Utilization:

ρ = λ / μ

= 15 / 20

= 0.75


Interpretation:

. Server utilization = 75%
. Counter is busy 75% of time.


If utilization > 1:

System unstable


If utilization < 1:

System stable
