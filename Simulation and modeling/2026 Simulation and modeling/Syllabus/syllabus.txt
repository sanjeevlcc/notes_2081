1. COURSE INTRODUCTION
============================================================

. Simulation and Modeling is a subject that helps students understand,
  design, analyze, and evaluate real-world systems using models.

. A model is a simplified representation of a real system.

. Simulation is the process of running or experimenting with that model
  to observe system behavior over time.

. This course teaches students how to solve real-world problems safely,
  efficiently, and economically.

. It is useful when testing the actual system is:
  . Expensive
  . Risky
  . Time-consuming
  . Difficult
  . Impractical

. The course focuses on:
  . Understanding real-life problems
  . Developing system models
  . Studying discrete systems
  . Studying continuous systems
  . Understanding random numbers
  . Applying Monte Carlo simulation
  . Simulating queuing systems
  . Modeling software development processes
  . Verifying and validating simulation models
  . Analyzing simulation output

. After completing this course, students will be able to create
  representative system models and study their performance over time
  and space.






============================================================
2. COURSE DESCRIPTION
============================================================

. This course is designed to solve real-world problems in a safe and
  efficient way.

. It supports the exploration of valuable solutions by providing clear
  insight into complex systems.

. The course deals with understanding problems from real life and
  developing solution models using discrete and continuous system
  properties.

. It also studies the nature and use of random numbers in simulation.

. The course introduces simulation of software development processes.

. It explains verification and validation of models to ensure that a
  model is correct, useful, and reliable.

. It also introduces emerging concepts such as:
  . Multi-agent based modeling
  . Software process simulation modeling
  . System dynamics simulation

. Simulation and Modeling helps in analyzing, designing, and evaluating
  complex systems by replicating real or proposed systems using computer
  software.

. It is especially useful when making changes in the actual system is
  difficult, costly, risky, or impractical.






============================================================
3. GENERAL OBJECTIVES
============================================================

. To acquaint students with the idea that system solutions can be tested
  more safely and cheaply using models than in the real world.

. To develop students' skills to test whether a product or system works
  before actually building it.

. To help students identify hidden or implicit issues in a system.

. To develop students' ability to detect and respond to system behavior.

. To help students successfully develop system models.

. To evaluate the process of decision making in crisis situations.

. To provide students with opportunities to absorb knowledge and practice
  skills in a realistic but simulated environment.

. To enable students to analyze and evaluate system performance through
  simulation.

6. LIST OF PRACTICALS
============================================================

. Practical activities should cover the essential concepts of the
  course.

. Students must actively participate in laboratory work.

. Students must submit practical reports to the instructor for final
  evaluation.

. Practical activities may be performed using:
  . Graphics programs
  . Open-source mapping tools
  . High-level programming languages
  . Simulation tools
  . Statistical tools

------------------------------------------------------------
Practical 1: Generation of Random Numbers Using Computer
------------------------------------------------------------

. Generate random numbers using computer programs.

. Use algorithms or built-in functions related to randomness.

. Understand pseudo-random and pseudo-independent numbers.

. Interpret generated output with real applications.

------------------------------------------------------------
Practical 2: Hypothesis Testing Using One-Sample Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test
------------------------------------------------------------

. Simulate and test the correctness of a hypothesis.

. Check whether data in vector x comes from a standard normal
  distribution.

. Compare the result against an alternative hypothesis.

. Interpret the statistical output.

------------------------------------------------------------
Practical 3: Monte Carlo Simulation
------------------------------------------------------------

. Perform Monte Carlo simulation for a complex system.

. Vary parameters within statistical constraints.

. Apply simulation to systems such as:
  . Financial models
  . Physical models
  . Mathematical models
  . Risk models

. Run simulation repeatedly in loops.

. Analyze the results to determine system characteristics.

------------------------------------------------------------
Practical 4: Conceptual Map of Traffic Model
------------------------------------------------------------

. Create a complete conceptual map of a traffic model.

. Include factors that increase or decrease traffic speed.

. Consider real-life traffic situations.

. Important factors may include:
  . Number of vehicles
  . Road width
  . Traffic light timing
  . Pedestrian movement
  . Vehicle speed
  . Road condition
  . Accidents
  . Weather condition

------------------------------------------------------------
Practical 5: Simulation of Single Server Queuing System
------------------------------------------------------------

. Simulate a single server queuing system.

. Study customer arrival and service process.

. Calculate performance measures such as:
  . Average waiting time
  . Average service time
  . Queue length
  . Server utilization
  . Idle time
  . Total time spent in system

. Apply the model to real examples such as:
  . Bank counter
  . Hospital registration desk
  . Ticket counter
  . Coffee shop order counter

------------------------------------------------------------
Practical 6: Simulation of Software Development Process
------------------------------------------------------------

. Simulate software development models and processes.

. Include the dynamics of software development.

. Explain:
  . Why simulation is needed in software engineering
  . What software process simulation is
  . How simulation is applied in software engineering

. Review and update the current state of Software Process Simulation
  Modeling, SPSM.

. Prepare a complete overview of SPSM.
