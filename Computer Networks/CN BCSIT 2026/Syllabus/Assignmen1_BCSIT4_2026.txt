CASE STUDY 1: 

DATA COMMUNICATION IN SMART COLLEGE NETWORK

(Unit I + Unit III)
============================================================
      
      Crimson College of Technology recently expanded its IT
      infrastructure to support online classes, smart labs, digital
      library services, and campus-wide internet access. The college
      management decided to build a modern communication system for
      efficient data transfer between departments, computer labs,
      library, and administration.
      
      The network design included both wired and wireless communication.
      For wired communication, Cat6 Ethernet cables and optical fiber
      were used. Fiber optic cables connected major buildings such as
      the main administrative block, library, and computer labs due to
      their high speed and low signal loss. Cat6 cables were used inside
      labs and offices to connect desktop computers and printers.
      
      For wireless communication, Wi-Fi access points based on IEEE
      802.11 standard were installed throughout the campus to provide
      internet access to students and faculty. Bluetooth devices were
      also used for short-range communication such as file sharing and
      peripheral connectivity.
      
      The IT team carefully considered data transmission modes. Online
      lectures mostly used full-duplex communication since teachers and
      students needed to send and receive data simultaneously. Security
      camera systems used simplex communication because video data moved
      only from camera to monitoring center.
      
      The network also faced physical layer challenges. During peak
      hours, students reported slow internet speed and video buffering.
      The IT team identified several transmission impairments such as
      attenuation in long cable connections, noise from nearby electrical
      equipment, and signal distortion due to poor cable quality.
      
      To improve performance, the college implemented multiplexing
      techniques to efficiently utilize communication channels.
      Bandwidth, throughput, latency, and jitter were also monitored to
      measure overall network performance. The team upgraded some older
      Fast Ethernet switches to Gigabit Ethernet switches for better
      speed and performance.
      
      After optimization, the college achieved faster internet speed,
      better connectivity, reduced packet loss, and smoother online
      learning experience.

CASE STUDY 2 : SMART ONLINE EDUCATION NETWORK SYSTEM
(Unit I + Unit VI + Unit VII + Unit VIII)
============================================================
      
      Crimson College of Technology recently launched a Smart Online
      Education System to support online classes, digital learning,
      online exams, and virtual lab activities for BCSIT students.
      
      The system allows students and teachers to communicate through
      video conferencing, online learning portals, email, and file
      sharing systems. Since large amounts of data are transmitted
      daily, efficient data communication became very important.
      
      The college network uses both wired and wireless communication.
      For live classes, data transmission happens in full-duplex mode,
      where teachers and students can send and receive voice, video,
      and messages simultaneously. High-speed internet and reliable
      communication channels are required to minimize delays.
      
      At the transport layer, the college uses different protocols for
      different services. TCP is used for web portals, online exams,
      and result systems because reliable communication is required.
      UDP is used for live video conferencing because speed is more
      important than retransmission delay.
      
      During exam periods, network congestion increased significantly
      due to thousands of students accessing the system at the same
      time. The IT team implemented congestion control mechanisms and
      traffic shaping algorithms such as Leaky Bucket and Token Bucket
      to manage traffic efficiently.
      
      At the application layer, several protocols were used. HTTP and
      HTTPS were used for accessing the college LMS portal. DNS helped
      convert domain names into IP addresses. SMTP, POP3, and IMAP were
      used for email communication. DHCP dynamically assigned IP
      addresses to students and faculty devices connecting to the
      campus network.
      
      Since the entire system handles sensitive data such as student
      records, exam questions, and financial details, network security
      became a major concern. The IT team deployed firewalls, VPN, and
      proxy servers to secure communication. Encryption techniques were
      used to protect confidential data during transmission.
      
      The security team also identified common threats such as phishing,
      malware, DDoS attacks, and unauthorized access attempts. To
      counter these threats, strong authentication, access control, and
      network monitoring systems were implemented.
      
      After implementing proper communication, transport protocols,
      application services, and security mechanisms, the college
      successfully improved system performance, security, and user
      experience.
