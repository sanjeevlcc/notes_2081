1. Course Description

The course emphasizes the practical application of MIS concepts through hands-on projects,
covering the complete lifecycle of MIS projects, from planning and design to implementation
and evaluation. Students will engage in real-world scenarios, developing essential skills in
project management, system design, data analysis, and more. The practicum culminates in the
presentation and evaluation of a final project, integrating theoretical knowledge with practical
experience. The course structure covers:

Practical application of Management Information Systems (MIS) concepts
Focus on hands-on projects covering the MIS project lifecycle
Development of project management, system design, and data analysis skills
Real-world scenarios and experiential learning
Culmination in a final project presentation and evaluation






2. General Objectives

The primary objective of this course is to equip MBA students with practical skills and
knowledge in the field of Management Information Systems. By engaging in hands-on projects
and real-world applications, students will:

To familiarize students with the role and importance of MIS in organizations
To enhance understanding of project planning, management, and execution within an MIS context
To develop skills in gathering and analyzing system requirements
To make students proficient in system design, development, and data management techniques
To equip students to effectively communicate technical information through presentations and reports
To prepare students for practical challenges in the implementation and testing of information systems






3. Contents in Detail

Management Information Systems Practicum typically focuses on providing practical
experience in the field of MIS. It combines theoretical knowledge with real-world
application, allowing students to work on projects, case studies, and potentially real business
scenarios. The trimester system regulations of Pokhara University for master's level
programs under the Faculty of Management Studies (FMS) grant autonomy to faculty
members in developing course details and preparing teaching plans. Faculty members are
empowered to adapt and modify course content to align with market needs and contextual
demands, provided that the core objectives and major components of the course remain
intact. The suggested course contents are as follows:






Unit I: Data Collection, Cleaning, and Preparation (3 Hours)

Objective:
Understand how to collect, clean, and prepare data for analysis.

1.1 Provide a dataset (e.g., sales data, customer data)
1.2 Students will use a tool like Excel or Power BI to clean the dataset
by removing duplicates, handling missing values, and normalizing
the data for analysis
1.3 Perform basic descriptive statistics (mean, median, mode) and
summary statistics (e.g., pivot tables in Excel or Power BI)
1.4 Deliverable: Students submit a cleaned dataset along with a summary
report showing key trends or patterns identified during cleaning






Unit II: Data Visualization with Power BI or any other available tool (3 Hours)

Objective:
Create interactive dashboards to visualize business performance.

2.1 Students will import the cleaned dataset into Power BI
2.2 Use Power BI’s visualization tools (e.g., bar charts, line charts, pie
charts, maps) to create a dynamic, interactive dashboard
2.3 Incorporate slicers and filters for different business perspectives
(e.g., sales by region, product category performance)
2.4 Emphasize storytelling with data and provide actionable business
insights
2.5 Deliverable: An interactive Power BI dashboard and a report
summarizing the key insights derived from the visualizations






Unit III: Business Intelligence Tools for Decision-Making (3 Hours)

Objective:
Use BI tools to analyze data for business decision-making.

3.1 Introduce a business scenario (e.g., analyzing customer churn,
optimizing sales strategies)
3.2 Students will use BI tools such as Tableau or Power BI to build a
report that incorporates KPIs, forecasting, and what-if analysis
3.3 Use DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) in Power BI or similar
formulas in Tableau to create measures and calculated fields that
reflect business performance metrics
3.4 Deliverable: A business report that includes forecasting trends,
decision-making recommendations, and an explanation of how the
BI tool helped in deriving these insights






Unit IV: Big Data Analytics and Business Applications (3 Hours)

4.1 Provide students with a sample big data set (e.g., large-scale retail
data, customer purchase behavior)
4.2 Introduce big data analytics concepts like Hadoop, Apache Spark, or
cloud-based analytics platforms (AWS, Google Cloud)
4.3 Use Power BI or Excel with external data sources to analyze trends
such as customer segmentation, market basket analysis, or sales
optimization
4.4 Deliverable: A project report showing the application of big data
analytics tools and techniques, along with insights and business
recommendations

Note:
The figures in the parentheses indicate the approximate teaching hours for the respective units.

5. Evaluation System and Students’ Responsibilities

5.1 Evaluation System

Both internal and external evaluations will be based on attendance, assignment, class
participation, self-reflection, project work, and presentation of the project report learning and
practicals. The faculty will provide final grades of the students. Thus, there will be no trimester
end examination and all evaluations will be conducted continuously throughout the trimester.

The evaluation process will consider the clarity and detail of the project plan, the thoroughness
of the requirements document, and the quality of the system prototype and testing. The final
presentation will be assessed for its communication effectiveness and organization. Participation
and engagement throughout the project will also be taken into account.

5.2 Students’ Responsibilities

Students are advised to attend all the classes/sessions and complete all the assignments within
the specified time period. If a student does not attend the sessions, it is his/her sole responsibility
to cover the topic(s) taught during the period. If a student fails to attend a formal exam, quiz,
test, etc. and there is not any provision for a re-exam.
