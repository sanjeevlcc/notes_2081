1. INTRODUCTION
................................................................................
The growth of information and communication technology (ICT) research has resulted
in a large body of literature. Researchers now face challenges in identifying
reliable sources, synthesizing findings, analyzing gaps, and presenting meaningful
conclusions. Writing a review article requires systematic topic selection,
literature screening, critical evaluation, and evidence-based reasoning.
This synopsis outlines a structured approach to developing a high-quality review
article using established research methodology principles.

- To outline best practices in academic writing, referencing, and avoiding

- To provide guidelines for discussion, conclusion, and future research writing.

4. METHODOLOGY

4.1 Literature Identification
- Use keywords relevant to ICT.
- Search digital libraries and databases.
- Apply Boolean operators and inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- Focus on peer-reviewed and credible publications.

4.2 Literature Screening and Appraisal
- Evaluate each articleâ€™s methodology, reliability, and relevance.
- Categorize papers according to themes, domains, approaches, or datasets.

4.3 Thematic Synthesis
- Group findings into conceptual clusters.
- Identify similarities, differences, trends, and contradictions.

4.4 Evidence Construction
- Compare reported results and validation metrics.
- Analyze strengths, weaknesses, limitations, and contributions.

4.5 Gap Analysis and Critical Review
- Identify unexplored areas, methodological shortcomings, and research
